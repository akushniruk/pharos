Why observable agents

Most agent tools optimize for output: faster diffs, more autonomy, bigger context windows. They undersell observability: what is running, on whose machine, with what permissions, and what changed while you were not looking. That gap creates mistrust, review friction, and “I don’t know what this agent did” moments — at exactly the scale where automation should reduce risk, not add it.

We exist to make AI coding agents observable and understandable: you can see activity, reason about it, and stay in control — so you ship with confidence on your machine and your codebase.

Differentiation vs generic agent tooling

  • Transparency over black-box autonomy — clarity beats “it just works” when the stakes are your source tree and credentials.
  • Understandability as a product surface — not only logs for experts, but narratives and signals a human can scan in one pass.
  • Confidence, not just speed — aligned with teams who treat agents as production participants, not demos.
